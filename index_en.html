Adrien Cloutier

PhD Candidate in Political Science | Université Laval | Defense Fall 2026

Adrien Cloutier is a PhD candidate in Political Science at Université Laval (FRQ funding), where his dissertation "Breaking News: Radar+ for Measuring Media Salience in the Digital Age" explores political communication, agenda-setting, media salience, and polarization using computational methods and open science. Defense expected in Fall 2026.

He is co-creator of the École interdisciplinaire outils & méthodes (EIOM), a summer school that brings together students, researchers, and professionals each year. Scientific coordinator of the Réseau francophone international en conseil scientifique (RFICS) from 2022 to 2024, he led international governance initiatives, organized a workshop in Dakar (July 2024), and structured partnerships connecting the academic world and public decision-makers in the Americas, Africa, and Europe.

Adrien is the creator of Radar+, a computational infrastructure developed entirely in R that automatically archives the homepages of ~20 Quebec, Canadian, and American media outlets every 10 minutes since 2019. Radar+ uses a local language model (LLM) to identify media objects (issues, actors, institutions, places) and develops absolute and relative salience indices (0-100). This infrastructure is at the core of his dissertation and postdoctoral project.

Lecturer at Université Laval since 2024, he created and teaches the course "Digital Tools in Social Sciences" (POL-6078), in addition to regularly teaching quantitative methodology. He also holds a Scrum Master certification and shares Notion templates for academic project management. His expertise lies at the intersection of research, pedagogy, and the development of computational infrastructures.

In 2027, he will begin a postdoctoral project at UCLA under the supervision of Professor Stuart Soroka, entitled "Fox vs CNN: A Longitudinal Analysis of Media Salience and Polarization in the United States". This project will analyze the American media landscape from 2019 to 2027 and develop a media divergence/concordance index to measure polarization.

CLESSN / CAPP

Adrien Cloutier is co-coordinator of two research centers at Université Laval, where he structures activities, supports research projects, and contributes to the development of digital infrastructures.

CLESSN

Chair in Digital Social Science Leadership
Center dedicated to pedagogical and methodological innovation in social sciences. CLESSN develops digital tools, trains in computational methods, and coordinates collaborative research initiatives. Adrien co-coordinates research activities, including the development of Radar+ and the structuring of interdisciplinary projects.

CAPP

Related Projects

As part of his co-coordination activities, Adrien contributes to several innovative projects:

Datagotchi

Datagotchi

Playful and educational application for exploring data interactively. Datagotchi aims to make data accessible to the general public and foster citizen participation through visualization and exploration of open data. A knowledge translation tool that connects research and society.

Quorum

Projet Quorum

Public participation device deployed at the Musée de la civilisation allowing citizens to deliberate on public issues. Projet Quorum combines political science research and citizen science to foster democratic engagement. A concrete example of applied research in partnership with cultural institutions.
